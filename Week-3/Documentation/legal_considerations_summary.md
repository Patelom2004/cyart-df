**Warrant Requirement Example**
Police suspect illegal financial activity and seize a suspect’s laptop.

The warrant specifically authorizes searching for:
1. Financial fraud documents
2. Transaction records

During analysis, investigators discover unrelated personal content, 
**The forensic examiner:**
1. Stops reviewing unrelated material
2. Reports findings to investigators
3. Requests additional judicial approval if needed

**Result**
If investigators search beyond the warrant scope:
1. Evidence may become “fruit of the poisonous tree”
2. Court can reject the evidence


                                                            **Real Court Case Example**
**Incident**
Investigators created a complete forensic **image of a suspect’s hard drive** during a financial investigation,The warrant **only allowed access to specific accounting files**.

**However:**
-> The government retained the full hard drive image for several years
-> Non-relevant personal files were also kept

**Court Ruling**
<ins>The court ruled that</ins>:- Retaining unrelated personal data beyond the warrant scope violated Fourth Amendment protections.

**Lesson Learned**
<ins> Forensic investigators should: </ins>
1. Collect only necessary evidence
2. Avoid retaining unrelated personal data
3. Delete or isolate non-relevant information early

**Final Outcome**
<ins>Because legal procedures were followed correctly:</ins>
1. Evidence remained admissible
2. Privacy laws were respected
3. Investigation remained legally valid in court
